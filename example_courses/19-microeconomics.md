## Scarcity and Choice

The fundamental problem of economics is scarcity: humans have unlimited wants but limited resources. This forces us to make choices.

## Opportunity Cost

Opportunity cost is the value of the next best alternative given up when making a choice.

Example:
If you spend 1 hour studying Python, the opportunity cost might be 1 hour of sleep or 1 hour of exercise.

## The Demand Curve

The law of demand states that, all else being equal (ceteris paribus), as the price of a good increases, the quantity demanded decreases. This creates a downward-sloping demand curve.

## Determinants of Demand

Demand is not just about price. It can shift due to:
- **Income**: Normal goods increase in demand as income rises.
- **Tastes and Preferences**: Trends can increase demand.
- **Prices of Related Goods**:
    - *Substitutes*: If the price of tea rises, demand for coffee may increase.
    - *Complements*: If the price of printers rises, demand for ink cartridges may decrease.

## The Law of Supply

Unlike demand, the law of supply states that as the price of a good increases, the quantity that producers are willing to supply also increases.

## Market Equilibrium

Equilibrium occurs where the quantity demanded equals the quantity supplied. The price at this intersection is the equilibrium price.

- **Shortage**: When price is below equilibrium, demand exceeds supply.
- **Surplus**: When price is above equilibrium, supply exceeds demand.
