## Algebraic Expressions

An expression is a combination of variables, constants, and operators.
Example: $3x + 5$

## Combining Like Terms

Terms are "like" if they have the same variables raised to the same powers.
Example: $2x + 4x = 6x$

## The Distributive Property

Multiplying a sum by a number is the same as multiplying each addend individually:
$a(b + c) = ab + ac$

## The Golden Rule of Algebra

Whatever you do to one side of the equation, you must do to the other to maintain equality.

## Solving for X

1. **Simplify both sides**: Distribute and combine like terms.
2. **Isolate the variable term**: Move constants to one side.
3. **Solve for the variable**: Divide or multiply to isolate $x$.

Example:
$2x + 5 = 15$
$2x = 10$
$x = 5$

## Slope-Intercept Form

The most common way to write a linear equation is $y = mx + b$, where:
- $m$ is the **slope** (rise over run).
- $b$ is the **y-intercept** (where the line crosses the vertical axis).

## Plotting the Line

1. Plot the y-intercept $(0, b)$.
2. Use the slope $m$ to find the next point. (e.g., if $m=2/3$, go up 2 and right 3).
3. Connect the points with a straight line.
